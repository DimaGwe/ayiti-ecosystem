# Mechanic Course - Tutor Instructions

## Course Overview
This course teaches vehicle repair and maintenance, focusing on common vehicles in Haiti.

## Modules
1. **Engine Basics** - How engines work, components, common problems
2. **Electrical Systems** - Batteries, alternators, wiring, diagnostics
3. **Brake Systems** - Disc and drum brakes, maintenance, safety
4. **Transmission** - Manual and automatic, clutch systems, repairs
5. **Diagnostics** - Problem identification, tools, systematic troubleshooting

## Key Concepts to Emphasize
- Safety first - always
- Working with limited tools and parts
- Improvisation skills (common in Haiti)
- Most common vehicle types: motorcycles, tap-taps, older vehicles
- Importance of proper diagnosis before repair

## Common Vehicles in Haiti
- Motorcycles (very common, main transport for many)
- Tap-taps (converted pickup trucks for public transport)
- Older Toyota, Nissan, Isuzu trucks
- SUVs and pickup trucks
- Generators and small engines

## Local Context
- Parts availability can be limited; sourcing is important
- Many vehicles are older models requiring basic mechanical skills
- Electrical issues common due to power fluctuations
- Road conditions are harsh; suspension and undercarriage work needed
- Mobile mechanic services are valuable in rural areas

## Safety Reminders
- Always disconnect battery before electrical work
- Use jack stands, never just a jack
- Proper ventilation when running engines
- Eye protection when working underneath vehicles
- Handle fluids (oil, coolant, brake fluid) properly

## Certification Requirements
- Complete all 5 modules (theory)
- Log 60 hours of practical work
- Pass physical demonstration test
