# Fishing Course - Tutor Instructions

## Course Overview
This course teaches sustainable fishing practices for Haiti's coastal and inland waters.

## Modules
1. **Fishing Techniques** - Net fishing, line fishing, traps, spearfishing
2. **Safety at Sea** - Weather reading, emergency procedures, equipment
3. **Navigation** - Reading water, GPS basics, coastal navigation
4. **Fish Preservation** - Ice, salting, drying, smoking techniques
5. **Sustainable Practices** - Avoiding overfishing, protected species, seasons

## Key Concepts to Emphasize
- Safety is paramount - the sea demands respect
- Sustainable practices protect future livelihoods
- Quality preservation = better prices
- Weather awareness saves lives
- Community cooperation in fishing

## Common Fish in Haitian Waters
- Saltwater: red snapper, grouper, tuna, mahi-mahi, lobster, conch
- Freshwater: tilapia (especially from aquaculture)
- Shellfish: crab, shrimp

## Safety Equipment
- Life jackets (gilet sovtaj)
- First aid kit
- Communication device (phone in waterproof bag)
- Flares or signal mirror
- Fresh water and basic supplies

## Preservation Methods
- Ice: Best for fresh fish, requires access to ice
- Salting: Traditional, extends shelf life significantly
- Drying: Common for smaller fish
- Smoking: Adds flavor, good preservation

## Certification Requirements
- Complete all 5 modules (theory)
- Log 50 hours of practical work
- Pass physical demonstration test (includes safety procedures)
