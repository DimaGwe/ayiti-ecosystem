# Boat Building Course - Tutor Instructions

## Course Overview
This course teaches traditional and modern boat construction techniques used in Haiti.

## Modules
1. **Wood Selection** - Types of wood, properties, sourcing sustainably
2. **Hull Design** - Traditional designs, measurements, stability principles
3. **Joinery Techniques** - Joints, fastenings, traditional methods
4. **Waterproofing** - Caulking, sealants, paint, maintenance
5. **Rigging & Finishing** - Masts, sails, outboard mounting, final touches

## Key Concepts to Emphasize
- Traditional Haitian boat-building knowledge is valuable
- Quality construction = safety at sea
- Sustainable wood sourcing is important
- Maintenance extends boat life significantly
- Both traditional and modern techniques have value

## Woods Used in Haiti
- Mahogany (kajou) - Premium, strong, rot-resistant
- Cedar - Light, rot-resistant
- Oak - Strong for frames
- Pine - Available but needs more protection
- Note: Sustainable sourcing is critical due to deforestation

## Tools
- Traditional: adze, hand planes, chisels, hand drills
- Modern: power tools when available
- Measuring: tape measure, level, string lines
- Students should learn both traditional and modern methods

## Waterproofing Methods
- Traditional caulking with cotton and pitch
- Modern marine sealants
- Bottom paint for protection
- Regular maintenance schedule

## Local Context
- Boat builders are respected craftsmen
- Skills passed down through generations
- Demand for repairs and new boats is steady
- Can work independently or for boat yards
- Tourism creates demand for quality boats

## Certification Requirements
- Complete all 5 modules (theory)
- Log 80 hours of practical work (longest requirement)
- Pass physical demonstration test
